# artistrax Pricing Strategy Models
## Comparing 5 Different Pricing Approaches

---

## Current Model (Recommended)

### Subscription + Platform Fee

**Artists:**
- 30-day free trial
- $20/mo or $96/year (60% off)
- Keep 95% of sales
- Platform takes 5%

**Labels:**
- 30-day free trial
- $25/mo or $120/year (60% off)
- Keep 90% of sales
- Platform takes 10%

**Fans:**
- 100% free
- No subscription
- Just pay for tracks ($1-2 each)

**Pros:**
- ✅ Predictable recurring revenue (90% of income)
- ✅ High margins (99%+ after Year 1)
- ✅ Low CAC ($12.50 per artist)
- ✅ Filters serious artists (reduces spam)
- ✅ Platform fees are bonus revenue

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Barrier to entry (need credit card)
- ⚠️ Churn risk (artists may cancel)
- ⚠️ Subscription fatigue (another monthly bill)

**Revenue Projection (Year 1):**
- Subscription: $26,100
- Platform fees: $3,600
- **Total: $29,700**

## Model A: Free for Artists (Bandcamp-style)

### Commission-Only Model

**Artists:**
- 100% free to list
- No subscription
- artistrax takes 18% (like Bandcamp)
- Artists keep 82%

**Labels:**
- 100% free to list
- artistrax takes 18%
- Labels keep 82%

**Fans:**
- 100% free
- Pay for tracks

**Pros:**
- ✅ Zero barrier to entry
- ✅ Easy to attract artists
- ✅ No churn (no subscription to cancel)
- ✅ Scales with sales (more sales = more revenue)

**Cons:**
- ❌ Unpredictable revenue (depends on sales)
- ❌ Lower margins (only make money on sales)
- ❌ Spam risk (no filter for serious artists)
- ❌ Need much higher sales volume to be profitable

**Revenue Projection (Year 1):**
- GMV: $60,000
- Platform fee (18%): $10,800
- Subscription: $0
- **Total: $10,800** (64% less than current model)

**Break-even:** Need $27K in GMV to cover costs
- Current model: Break-even at Q4
- This model: Break-even at ~$150K GMV (Year 2)

## Model B: Higher Subscription, No Platform Fee

### All-Subscription Model

**Artists:**
- 30-day free trial
- $30/mo or $180/year
- Keep 100% of sales
- No platform fee

**Labels:**
- 30-day free trial
- $40/mo or $240/year
- Keep 100% of sales
- No platform fee

**Fans:**
- 100% free

**Pros:**
- ✅ Artists keep 100% (strongest value prop)
- ✅ Predictable revenue (all subscription)
- ✅ Simpler messaging ("keep everything")
- ✅ High LTV (more per artist)

**Cons:**
- ❌ Higher price = lower conversion
- ❌ Higher CAC (need more marketing to convince)
- ❌ Churn risk increases (higher monthly cost)
- ❌ No revenue if artist has no sales

**Revenue Projection (Year 1):**
- Artists: 350 × $30 × 9 months avg = $94,500
- Labels: 50 × $40 × 9 months avg = $18,000
- **Total: $112,500** (3.8x higher!)

**But:**
- Conversion rate drops 30-50% (higher price)
- Actual signups: 200 artists, 30 labels
- Actual revenue: $54,000 + $10,800 = $64,800

**Trade-off:** Higher price, fewer users

## Model C: Freemium Model

### Free + Premium Tiers

**Free Tier (Artists):**
- Upload 3 tracks max
- Keep 85% (artistrax takes 15%)
- Basic analytics
- No Link-in-Bio

**Premium Tier (Artists):**
- $20/mo or $96/year
- Unlimited uploads
- Keep 95% (artistrax takes 5%)
- Advanced analytics
- Link-in-Bio included

**Labels:**
- Free: 10 tracks max, keep 85%
- Premium: $25/mo, unlimited, keep 90%

**Pros:**
- ✅ Lower barrier (free tier attracts users)
- ✅ Upsell path (convert free to paid)
- ✅ More total users (free + paid)
- ✅ Network effects (more catalog = more fans)

**Cons:**
- ❌ Complex pricing (confusing)
- ❌ Support burden (free users need help)
- ❌ Revenue dilution (free users don't pay)
- ❌ Spam risk (free tier abuse)

**Revenue Projection (Year 1):**
- Free artists: 200 (no subscription)
- Premium artists: 150 × $20 × 9 = $27,000
- Platform fees (free): 200 × $50 × 15% = $1,500
- Platform fees (premium): 150 × $50 × 5% = $375
- **Total: $28,875** (similar to current model)

**Trade-off:** More users, similar revenue, higher costs

**Trade-off:** Higher ARPU, fewer users

## Pricing Psychology

### Why $20/mo Works

**Anchoring:**
- Spotify: $11/mo (consumers used to this)
- $20/mo seems reasonable for tools + 95% share

**Value perception:**
- Bandcamp: Free but takes 18% ($18 on $100 sale)
- artistrax: $20/mo but takes 5% ($5 on $100 sale)
- **Break-even: $111 in sales ($111 × 18% = $20)**

**Framing:**
- "Only $20/mo to keep 95% vs 82%"
- "One sale of $20 pays for the month"
- "Less than a coffee per day"

---

### Why $96/year Works (60% Off)

**Discount perception:**
- $240/year → $96/year = massive savings
- "Save $144" (sounds huge)
- Anchoring effect (compare to monthly)

**Lock-in:**
- Annual = 12-month commitment
- Lower churn (already paid)
- Better for cash flow

**Messaging:**
- "Only $8/month when paid annually"
- "Two months free!"
- "60% off annual plan"

## Pricing Decision Framework

### When to Change Pricing

**Increase prices if:**
- Churn <10% (users love it)
- CAC declining (word of mouth strong)
- Margin >70% (room to give more to artists)
- Competitors raise prices

**Decrease prices if:**
- Signups declining
- Churn >20%
- Competitors undercut
- Market feedback says "too expensive"

**Add tiers if:**
- Users requesting features beyond current plan
- Enterprise customers asking for white-label
- Freemium could drive network effects
